# Localis · Design Context

## Users

**Primary**: cruise passengers in Bari (4-6h port stop), reading on mobile outdoors with strong sun, walking the Old Town. Cultural tourists 2-3 day Bari/Puglia visit, secondary.

**Mental state**: explorer, curious, physically present in narrow alleys of Bari Vecchia. Wants stories not facts. Browser-only (resists app installs).

**Job to be done**: "I'm here. Make me feel why this place matters in 9 minutes, while I walk."

**Demographic mix**: 55% IT, 15% Polish, 6% Romanian, 5% French, 4% German, 15% other. Age 35-65 majority. Disposable income moderate. Smartphone-first.

## Brand Personality

**Three words**: lived-in · authoritative · cinematic

**Voice**: a barese intellectual who has seen and read everything about his city, but talks to you in a low voice over a glass of Primitivo — not lecturing, confiding. Not academic. Not touristy. Specific names, specific dates, specific smells.

**Anti-references**:
- ❌ Tourist brochure aesthetic (saturated colors, exclamation marks, "BENVENUTI A BARI!")
- ❌ Generic SaaS dashboard (sterile sans, blue gradients, drop shadows on cards)
- ❌ "Italian souvenir" stereotype (red-white-green, Comic Sans, hearts, "Mamma Mia")
- ❌ Generic podcast-app render (purple/cyan gradients, neon glow, Spotify-clone — bright violet UI). NB: phone mockups *per se* are allowed if rendered sober (matte black device, no 3D tilt, no glow, Localis UI inside) — see §Phone mockup policy.
- ❌ Cormorant Garamond + serif everywhere (exhausted reflex among editorial AI sites)

**Reference physical objects**:
- A 1960s Italian travel magazine (Domus, Epoca, Bell'Italia originale)
- The cover of a Mondadori Oscar paperback edition of Carlo Levi
- A handwritten ledger in a notary's office in Bari
- Bauhaus poster in Italian translation
- A Pugliese cookbook from a tiny independent publisher

**Emotion the interface should evoke**: confidence + intimacy. The opposite of overwhelm. "I am about to be told something I didn't know."

## Aesthetic Direction

**Mood**: editorial-modern. Magazine-grade, not website-grade.

**Palette** (OKLCH, perceptually uniform):
- **Surface base** (parchment): `oklch(96% 0.012 75)` — warm cream, not pure white. Hint of yellow-orange to feel sun-warmed paper.
- **Ink** (text primary): `oklch(20% 0.025 240)` — deep navy-black, tinted toward blue. Not pure black.
- **Accent terracotta**: `oklch(56% 0.14 45)` — pugliese tufa stone burned by sun. Used sparingly (logo accent, italic words, single CTA).
- **Sea blue** (secondary accent): `oklch(46% 0.085 240)` — muted Adriatic. For links, secondary CTAs.
- **Stone gray** (borders, dividers): `oklch(75% 0.008 75)` — surface tinted toward base hue, low chroma.
- **Warm beige** (subtle surfaces): `oklch(92% 0.018 75)` — for cards/elevated surfaces against parchment base.

**Theme**: light mode default. Reasons:
- Outdoor use under sun: dark mode = glare on phone screens
- Editorial reference: magazines are paper, not OLED
- Premium positioning: light + restraint reads as more refined than dark + glow
- Optional dark mode for night reading (hotel room): generated via OKLCH lightness inversion, NOT just inverted colors

**Typography** — Editorial Italiana del '60 (Spectral + Schibsted Grotesk):

- **Display**: **Spectral** by Production Type (Google Fonts free). Weights: 300 (display), 400 (regular), 500 (mid). Italic 300/400 used for emphasis (`<em>`, evocative phrases like "Mentre sei lì"). Letter-spacing: `-0.018em` on large headings, `-0.012em` on mid, none on smaller. Optical scale: heading sizes via `clamp()` fluid scale: `clamp(2.2rem, 5vw, 3.4rem)` for hero h1.

- **Body**: **Schibsted Grotesk** (free Google, by Schibsted media group). Weights: 400 (body), 500 (mid emphasis), 600 (labels), 700 (eyebrow caps). Letter-spacing: `0.22em` for `text-transform: uppercase` eyebrows + tags, normal spacing for body. Line-height: 1.65 body, 1.2 UI labels.

- **Pairing rationale**: Spectral italic carries Italian text well (descenders + ascenders + classical proportions). Schibsted Grotesk = humanist sans editoriale, distintivo, NON Inter-clone. Combinazione = "rivista italiana del '60" senza ricadere in Cormorant/Fraunces stereotype.

- **Type scale** (modular 1.25 ratio):
  - `--text-xs`: 0.75rem (12px) — eyebrows, microcopy
  - `--text-sm`: 0.875rem (14px) — secondary labels
  - `--text-base`: 1rem (16px) — body
  - `--text-lg`: 1.125rem (18px) — lead body
  - `--text-xl`: 1.25rem (20px) — h4
  - `--text-2xl`: 1.5rem (24px) — h3
  - `--text-3xl`: 2rem (32px) — h2
  - `--text-4xl`: clamp(2.2rem, 5vw, 3.4rem) — h1 (hero)
  - `--text-5xl`: clamp(2.8rem, 7vw, 4.8rem) — display (rare, for landing hook)

- **Italic policy**: italics ONLY on Spectral display, NEVER on Schibsted Grotesk. Use italic for emphatic narrative phrases ("Mentre sei lì.", "Storie vere."), never for Latin abbreviations or product names.

- **Body line length cap**: `max-width: 65ch` on prose blocks. Never let body wrap beyond ~75 char.

**Spacing**: 4pt scale (`4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96`). Generous breathing on hero/headings. Tighter on functional UI (player controls, chapters list). NEVER same padding everywhere.

**Layout**: asymmetric editorial grid. Hero left-aligned, NOT centered. Subtle break-the-grid moments for emphasis (a quote that bleeds into margin, a chapter number in the gutter).

## Design Principles (5)

1. **Restraint over decoration** — the page is mostly white space. The terracotta accent appears 1-3 times max per screen. Nothing is added "to fill space."
2. **Type does the work** — visual hierarchy comes from font weight + size + leading, not from boxes/cards/borders. A heading needs a heading, not a heading inside a card.
3. **Real specifics over abstractions** — "9 minuti, nei vicoli del Borgo Antico, fra mercatini e affreschi normanni" beats "Audio guides for Italian cities."
4. **Outdoor leg-test** — every screen must be readable at arm's length, on a 6" phone, in direct July sun. Light backgrounds + high contrast + minimum 16px body.
5. **Audio-first hierarchy** — the play button is the most important element on any page. Everything else supports it. Not the other way around.

## Constraints

- **Framework**: Astro 5 + Tailwind CSS (utility-first with custom design tokens)
- **Browser support**: Chrome/Safari/Firefox last 2 versions, mobile-first
- **Performance**: Lighthouse 90+ on mobile, < 100KB per page (excluding audio)
- **Accessibility**: WCAG AA minimum, AAA on body text. Screen reader + keyboard navigation.
- **i18n**: IT (default) + EN, hreflang correct, no language-switch via display:none anti-pattern.
- **PWA potential**: Service Worker caches audio for offline use after first play.
- **No tracking cookies**: Plausible analytics only (privacy-first, no banner needed).

## Phone mockup policy

Phone mockups are **allowed** in Localis marketing surfaces (hero, feature blocks, app-readiness cues) provided they obey:

- **Frame**: realistic matte black device silhouette, ~9:19.5 aspect ratio, hairline border (1px ink/15%), no shadow, no glow, no rim-light, no 3D perspective tilt. Static front-facing, optionally with a 0.5° subtle rotation only on lg viewports.
- **Bezel & notch**: thin uniform bezel (8-12px), dynamic-island slot. Do not draw the home indicator at the bottom — Localis is browser-first, the device is generic, not iOS-branded.
- **Inside (screen content)**: rendered HTML with Localis design tokens — Spectral title, Schibsted body, terracotta accent for play/progress, parchment surface tint. Never use a screenshot of another app. Never put a fake notification or "noise" UI to fill space.
- **Composition**: phone occupies a single grid cell; surrounding plate/photo/figure preserved if present. Never let the device dominate the hero — it lives alongside Italian editorial typography, not above it.
- **Anti-patterns** (still forbidden): purple/cyan gradients on or behind the device, neon ring, floating in mid-air with reflection, perspective shadow on parchment, pill-shaped CTA inside the device screen, equalizer animation that loops forever, pulsating play button.

Rationale: a sober mockup conveys "fits in your hand" without inheriting the SaaS-podcast register. The constraint is the device frame's *photographic tone* (matte product shot) — not its inclusion.

## Banned (project-specific)

- ❌ Cormorant Garamond (and any variant)
- ❌ Inter, IBM Plex, DM Sans/Serif, Plus Jakarta, Outfit, Instrument
- ❌ Lora, Fraunces, Newsreader, Crimson, Playfair Display, Syne
- ❌ Side-stripe borders (left/right colored borders >1px on cards/callouts)
- ❌ Gradient text (background-clip: text on text fill)
- ❌ Glassmorphism (blur+glass cards as decoration)
- ❌ Pure black `#000` or pure white `#fff` (always tint toward brand hue)
- ❌ Cyan-on-dark / purple-blue gradients (AI slop palette)
- ❌ Bouncy or elastic easing
- ❌ Modal dialogs except when truly unavoidable
